Palas Mall Accessibility Improvements

Location: Palas Mall

Main Personnas

  • Maria Popescu, high-school teacher
  • Alexandru Antal, store owner
Here is a more detailed overview of chosen personnas

Usability problems observed and potential improvements:

  • Inadequate signage for restrooms, exits, and stores:
    Improvement: Install larger signs with high-contrast colors, braille, and universally recognized symbols to help all visitors navigate more easily.
  • Limited seating areas, especially near high-traffic locations:
    Improvement: Add comfortable seating areas throughout the mall, with a focus on high-traffic locations and rest areas. Ensure these seating spaces have wheelchair accessible seating as well.
  • Touchscreen directory maps with small fonts and poor contrast:
    Improvement: Redesign the touchscreen directories to include larger fonts, high-contrast colors, and simplified graphics. Add auditory feedback to make the interface more accessible for visually impaired users.
  • Narrow aisles and inaccessible store entrances for wheelchair users:
    Improvement: Work with store owners to widen aisles and modify store entrances to accommodate wheelchair users. Implement accessible ramps and automatic doors where necessary.
  • No audible guidance for visually impaired visitors:
    Improvement: Introduce an audio-guidance system using strategically placed speakers, Bluetooth beacons, or a dedicated mobile app to provide directions and information for visually impaired visitors.

Additional software improvements:

  • Mobile app for personalized mall navigation:
    Develop a mobile app that offers personalized navigation, including accessible routes and turn-by-turn directions for all visitors, including those with special needs. The app can also provide real-time information on store promotions and events.
  • Indoor positioning system (IPS):
    Implement an indoor positioning system using Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or other technologies to help users locate themselves within the mall, enhancing their navigation experience.
  • Augmented reality (AR) support:
    Integrate augmented reality features into the mobile app to provide users with visual overlays that highlight important information, such as store names, accessible entrances, and other points of interest.
  • Conversational UI agent:
    Incorporate a conversational UI agent, such as a chatbot, into the mobile app to provide users with an alternative way to access information and ask questions, enhancing the overall user experience.

Empirical laws, affordances, and flow in the context of the improved solutions:

  • Fitts' Law: By making interactive elements, such as touchscreen directories and mobile app interfaces, more accessible and easier to use, visitors can interact with them more efficiently.
  • Hick's Law: Simplified navigation and reduced choices on the touchscreen directory and mobile app help users make decisions faster, resulting in a more streamlined experience.
  • Steering Law: Wider aisles, accessible store entrances, and improved navigation minimize the need for complex, precision-based movements, enabling users to move more easily through the mall.
  • Affordances: Clear visual cues, such as high-contrast signage and accessible route indicators, help users better understand the environment and navigate more effectively.
  • Flow: The combination of improved navigation, better accessibility, and enhanced usability create a more enjoyable and efficient experience for all users, including those with special needs. As a result, visitors will be more likely to have a positive experience and return to the shopping mall in the future.
